ISIS is a terrorist group to has “taken territory” in both the physical and digital spheres. ISIS places a high premium on public messaging to advance its objectives, thus setting up central media outlets for its messaging. All of this forms a complex network of content and actors whose goal is to leverage the power of the Internet to promote violent extremism. The Rami Method is a new approach to tackling violent extremism recruitment efforts online.

What is the Rami Method?

The Rami Method is a 4-step experiment that employs readily available online resources — existing online videos and targeted advertising tools — to counter ISIS recruitment efforts online, in an engaging, transparent and responsible way.

Four guiding principles have shaped this project and made it different from other initiatives underway:

Focus on those who are the most susceptible to ISIS’ messaging

We envisioned the slice of ISIS’ audience that was most susceptible to the group’s message and actively seeking to engage with ISIS-produced content. We started by speaking directly to those who had been recruited and convinced by ISIS rhetoric. Our main principle in developing a way to reach our target audience was ensuring people’s privacy: nothing in this experiment involves monitoring user data. Our targeting strategy therefore relied on using curated, effffective keywords to engage our audience online.

Meet your audience where they are, Not where you think they should be

Our campaign should be engaging, and never give our audience the feeling of being confronted by a preachy and agenda-driven content. This principle guided us across all stages of designing and implementing the pilot: in the video selection, the draftfting of advertisement text, the creation of the visual identity of the video channels, etc.

The most authentic and credible content is already out there

Our experiment did not aim to create new “anti-ISIS” content, or to rely on content produced by governments for the purpose of fighting ISIS’ messaging online. Rather, we sought out material uploaded by individuals and civil society organizations, and we found that citizens’ testimonies, reports, and religious debates served as the most credible and legitimate voices.

Openness & transparency will allow to improve and scale

Our experiment was not designed to develop a definitive “how-to” guide, but rather to begin a collaborative process whereby those engaging ISIS and affiliated groups online can learn from one another and build on each other’s successes. We are releasing the story of our experiment, the data, and the blueprint of how to replicate the project. We leave it to others to build upon, continue the conversation, and improve this approach.

The Team

Creating content, conducting research, running advertising campaigns, doing analytics: these are different areas of expertise that typically reside within distinct organizations. This Pilot has involved four researchers, videos from 83 different content creators (33 in English and 50 in Arabic), two lead partners, one market research firm and one technology incubator. We believe this model is particularly powerful.

Valens Global: conducted the main analytic effort to identify and categorize the most powerful existing online videos that refute important parts of ISIS’s message, and organize this material to make it readily deployable for the Pilot. Learn more about Valens Global

Moonshot CVE: coordinated the English-language campaign in close collaboration with Jigsaw; this involved the translation of research into coded databases, design and production of advertising content, creating advertising campaigns, developing targeting keywords, day-to-day campaign management and analysis of campaign results. Learn more about Moonshot CVE

Quantum Communications: coordinated the Arabic-language campaign in close collaboration with the rest of the partners; this involved the translation of research into coded databases, design and production of advertising content, creating advertising campaigns, developing targeting keywords, day-to-day campaign management and analysis of campaign results. Learn more about Quantum Communications

Jigsaw: provided the initial vision, recruited the partners, and incubated the project. Learn more about Jigsaw
